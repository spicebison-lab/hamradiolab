// TL;DR — Plain English Summary

Some links on this site are affiliate links. If you click one and buy something, I may earn a small commission — at no extra cost to you. I only link to gear I'd actually recommend. My opinions are always my own and are never influenced by whether a commission is involved.

01 What Is an Affiliate Link?

An affiliate link is a regular product link with a tracking code attached. When you click it and make a purchase, the retailer pays me a small referral commission. The price you pay is identical — affiliates are paid by the retailer, not by you.

This is a standard, FTC-recognized way for content creators to earn income without charging readers directly. Ham Radio Lab uses affiliate links to help cover hosting, equipment testing, and the time spent writing guides.

03 How It Affects My Reviews

Bluntly: it doesn't — or it shouldn't, and I work hard to make sure it doesn't. Here's how I keep it honest:

  • I only link to products I would genuinely recommend to a new ham or a friend
  • Negative aspects of gear are called out in reviews regardless of affiliate status
  • I never accept payment, free products, or sponsorships in exchange for positive coverage
  • Whether or not an affiliate program exists for a product has no bearing on whether I recommend it
  • If I think a cheaper or better product exists than the one with an affiliate link, I'll say so

Example: The Baofeng UV-5R earns almost nothing in commission due to its low price. I still recommend it as a first radio because it's genuinely useful for that purpose — not because of any financial incentive.
