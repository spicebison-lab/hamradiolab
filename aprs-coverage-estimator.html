RF Propagation Model

Coverage estimates use a modified Friis free-space path loss equation combined with a HAAT (Height Above Average Terrain) correction. The model accounts for terrain type via an empirical correction factor derived from ITU-R P.1546 data for VHF propagation at 144 MHz.

d = 4.12 × √(h_tx + h_digi)

The radio horizon formula above (in km) gives the optical line-of-sight distance. RF propagation at VHF extends roughly 15% beyond optical horizon due to atmospheric refraction (k-factor ≈ 1.33).

Path Hop Modeling

Each digipeater hop multiplies coverage by the digipeater's assumed HAAT. The model assumes digipeaters are located at elevated sites (typical HAAT: 50–150m) consistent with community-maintained infrastructure. Actual hop coverage depends heavily on digipeater placement in your area — verify at aprs.fi.

Coverage(n hops) = d_direct × multiplier^n

Terrain correction factors: Flat/Urban 1.0×, Suburban 0.85×, Hilly 0.65×, Mountain 0.45× — based on empirical path loss data for obstructed VHF environments.
