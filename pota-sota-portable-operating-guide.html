POTA and SOTA: The Ultimate Guide to Portable Operating

10 MIN READ
UPDATED MAR 2026

Parks on the Air (POTA) and Summits on the Air (SOTA) have become the most popular activities in ham radio today. They combine the thrill of amateur radio with the beauty of the outdoors, encouraging operators to get out of their shacks and into nature. Whether you're a seasoned operator or a newcomer, portable operating offers a unique challenge and a lot of fun.

What is POTA?

Parks on the Air (POTA) is a program that encourages amateur radio operators to activate parks (state, national, and international) by setting up a station and making contacts. It's a great way to test your portable gear and enjoy the outdoors. You can be an "activator" (the one in the park) or a "hunter" (the one trying to contact the activator).

  • **Accessibility:** Many parks are easy to access by car.
  • **Community:** A large and active community of hunters and activators.
  • **Awards:** Earn awards for activating different parks or making a certain number of contacts.

What is SOTA?

Summits on the Air (SOTA) is similar to POTA but focuses on activating mountain summits. It's generally more physically demanding, as it often involves hiking or climbing to reach the summit. SOTA is a fantastic way to combine ham radio with hiking and mountaineering.

  • **Challenge:** Requires lightweight gear and physical fitness.
  • **Unique Locations:** Activate summits that may have never been on the air before.
  • **Scoring:** Points are awarded based on the difficulty and elevation of the summit.

Essential Gear for Portable Operating

When going portable, weight and efficiency are key. Here's what you'll need:

  • **Portable Transceiver:** Radios like the Icom IC-705, Yaesu FT-818, or Elecraft KX2/KX3 are popular choices.
  • **Lightweight Antenna:** End-fed half-wave (EFHW) or vertical antennas are easy to pack and deploy.
  • **Power Source:** LiFePO4 batteries are lightweight and provide consistent power.
  • **Logging Tools:** A simple notebook or a smartphone app like HAMRS for logging contacts.
  • **Backpack:** A comfortable pack to carry all your gear.

Why You Should Try It

Portable operating is a fantastic way to improve your radio skills, test your equipment in different environments, and enjoy the beauty of nature. It's also a great way to meet other hams and be part of a vibrant community. So, grab your gear and head to a park or a summit!
