Solar Cycle 25: What it Means for Ham Radio Propagation

10 MIN READ
UPDATED MAR 2026

Solar Cycle 25 is currently in full swing, and it's bringing some of the best radio conditions we've seen in years. For amateur radio operators, this means more opportunities for long-distance (DX) communication, especially on the higher HF bands. Understanding how solar activity affects propagation is key to making the most of this cycle.

What is a Solar Cycle?

The Sun goes through an approximately 11-year cycle of activity, marked by the number of sunspots on its surface. More sunspots mean more solar radiation, which ionizes the Earth's atmosphere and creates the layers that reflect radio waves back to Earth. Solar Cycle 25 began in late 2019 and is expected to peak in the mid-2020s.

How Solar Activity Affects Propagation

Solar activity has a direct impact on the ionosphere, which in turn affects how radio waves propagate. Here's how it works:

  • **Sunspots:** More sunspots mean more ionization, which leads to better propagation on higher frequencies (like 10m, 12m, and 15m).
  • **Solar Flares:** Large solar flares can cause sudden ionospheric disturbances (SIDs), which can temporarily disrupt radio communication.
  • **Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs):** CMEs can trigger geomagnetic storms, which can lead to auroras and enhanced propagation on some bands, but can also cause radio blackouts.

What to Expect in Solar Cycle 25

Solar Cycle 25 has already exceeded many predictions, with sunspot numbers higher than expected. This means we can look forward to:

  • **Enhanced DX:** More opportunities to contact stations in distant countries on the higher HF bands.
  • **Better Band Openings:** Bands like 10m and 12m will be open more frequently and for longer periods.
  • **Increased Solar Activity:** More solar flares and CMEs, which can lead to both enhanced propagation and occasional disruptions.

How to Monitor Solar Activity

There are many online resources and tools to help you monitor solar activity and predict propagation conditions. Websites like SpaceWeather.com and NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center provide real-time data and forecasts. You can also use tools like VOACAP for more detailed propagation predictions.

Why You Should Be Excited

Solar Cycle 25 is a fantastic time to be an amateur radio operator. Whether you're a seasoned DXer or a newcomer, the enhanced propagation conditions offer endless opportunities for exploration and discovery. So, keep an eye on the sun and get on the air!
